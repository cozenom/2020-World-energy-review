Carbon Dioxide emission on a global scale

Carbon Dioxide is the gas emitted by humans. This includes gasses like methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases. These gasses get trapped in the atmosphere, causing global warming. As most people know the world is collectively trying to reduce Carbon Dioxide emissions to slow down the impact it has on global warming. Gas flaring is the practice of burning gas associated with oil extraction. this has been done since the beginning of oil production. This process is very wasteful and polluting to the environment but the major reason why we still do gas flaring is that it's relatively safe and the export of gas requires an economically viable market as the company doing the oil production needs to capture, transport, process, and sell the gas.

As we can see from the charts below on a World scale Carbon Dioxide emissions didn't really drop down over the last 20 years. We saw a drop in natural gas and total flaring in the 1980s but not from non-flaring emissions.
